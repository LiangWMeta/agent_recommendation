4. Key Findings

Findings from eCPM Investigation

7 Findings
1. pCTR (prod_prediction) is harmful for PM scoring
Separation ratio: 0.97x — negatives score higher than positives. Using pCTR for PM truncation gives 29.7% positive survival vs cosine's 48.6%. This signal should never be the primary PM scorer.
2. PM total_value (per-request) is 36.6x better
Where available (4% from old source), pm_total_value has 36.6x positive-negative separation. This is the ground truth PM scoring signal. Coverage was low because we were reading from an AP-level table.
3. fct_raa_consideration_data is the right table
RAA consideration data has pm_stage_struct.total_value at 63% coverage — the actual PM scoring output. Also provides is_forced_retrieval_ads flags, ad_rank_by_stage, and AI/AF total_values.
4. FR bypass now works with real prod flags
Old data had 0-1 FR ads per request. RAA data gives 55-136 FR ads — matching production behavior. These ads bypass PM truncation, preserving FR-unique positives that were previously lost.
5. Ad-level eCPM is weaker than user-specific signals
median_ecpm (2.48x separation) and median_pm_tv are ad-level aggregates across users. For PM truncation, user-specific cosine (which captures "is this ad relevant to THIS user") outperforms at the PM stage. Optimal solution: combine both.
6. Prod model route improved with eCPM scoring
Prod model unique positives: 3.5 (pCTR) → 6.6 (eCPM). eCPM scoring nearly doubles the prod model route's ability to find unique positive ads, validating the switch from pCTR to pm_total_value.
7. Next steps: user-specific PM total_value + combined scoring
The ideal PM scorer would be user-specific total_value (per-request PM scoring). To get this, query fct_raa_consideration_data with request_id joins, or build a combined scorer: cosine (user-specific) + median_pm_tv (ad quality) + cluster engagement (contextual).
